About

What Auralethi Is

Auralethi is a nonprofit organization focused on AI safety, specifically on a layer of risk that has received less attention than it deserves.

Most AI safety work examines whether systems behave correctly in isolated moments. Auralethi examines what happens when people interact with AI systems continuously, over time. That shift in focus changes what safety means, what it requires, and what it looks like when it is working.

Why It Exists

AI systems are no longer used occasionally. They are becoming part of how people think, decide, seek support, and understand their options. This kind of sustained interaction introduces risks that single-output evaluations were never designed to catch: subtle behavioral shaping, dependency formation, trust that accumulates faster than it should, and influence on decision-making that compounds over time.

These are not hypothetical concerns. They are the predictable consequence of deploying systems built for engagement without designing them for long-term human wellbeing.

The Perspective Shift

Traditional AI safety asks: does this system behave correctly right now?

Auralethi extends that question: what does this system do to a person across thousands of interactions? What does it shape, over time, in how that person thinks and decides and relies?

This is not a rejection of existing safety work. It is an extension of it, into territory that becomes more important as AI systems become more present in people's lives.

How Auralethi Works

The work moves across three connected areas:

Research. Developing frameworks for understanding relational risk in human-AI interaction, including the concept of Relational Surface Risk.

System Design. Building architectures that treat safety and alignment as structural properties, not post-hoc constraints.

Applied Models. Implementing and testing these ideas in real contexts, so the work remains grounded in practice rather than theory alone.

These are not separate tracks. They inform each other.

The Larger Goal

Auralethi's work is grounded in AI safety, but its aim is fundamentally human. The goal is not only to prevent harm. It is to ensure that the systems people rely on actually support their autonomy, their judgment, and their ability to participate meaningfully in their own lives.

Support systems that undermine the people they support are not safe, regardless of what their benchmarks say.

Where This Is Headed

This work is early. Auralethi is building carefully, one specific problem, understood precisely, addressed directly. The long-term direction extends toward integrated environments where support is designed holistically across digital and physical contexts. That work is exploratory and ongoing.

Founder

Auralethi was founded by James Dayson, a software engineer and system designer with experience building automation systems, workflow tools, and cognitive support software in real-world environments.
